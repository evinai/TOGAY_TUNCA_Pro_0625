Where to Start with AI: Talent, Taste, and the True Role of Artificial Intelligence

By: Togay Tunca and Claude Sonnet4 | June 12, 2025

Not Every Expert Is a Creator: The Wine and Engine Analogy

Consider This:

  • You can be a world-class wine grower—knowing everything about grapes, soil, and weather—yet lack the taste buds to recognize a truly great wine.
  • You might be an engine specialist, understanding every part and process, but still be a terrible driver.
  • Some have a great ear for music but cannot compose a memorable song.

These analogies reveal a simple truth: mastery in one area does not guarantee excellence in another, even if they are closely related. The same is true for AI.

AI Is Not Just for Mathematicians

Many believe that to work with AI, you must be a mathematical genius or a coding prodigy. In reality, AI is a tool—like a kitchen utensil for a chef or the grape itself for a winemaker. What matters most is your talent, your curiosity, and your urge to solve problems in your own domain.

Being part of the AI revolution is not about calculations—it's about your unique perspective, your interests, and your willingness to explore.

Start with What You Love

If you have a keen eye for something—be it art, business, science, or social issues—AI can become your partner in creation. The most important step is not learning every algorithm, but understanding the problems you care about and being open to new ways of solving them.

AI as a Partner, Not a Replacement

  • For a winemaker, AI can help analyze soil data, predict harvest times, or even suggest new blends—but it cannot replace the winemaker's taste and vision.
  • For a musician, AI can generate melodies or analyze trends, but it cannot feel the emotion behind a song.
  • For a business owner, AI can process vast amounts of data, but it is your understanding of your customers that makes the difference.

How to Begin Your AI Journey

  • Identify Your Domain: What are you passionate about? Where do you see problems or opportunities?
  • Frame the Problem: Clearly define what you want to solve or create. The better you understand your challenge, the more useful AI will be as a partner.
  • Explore AI Tools: Start with simple, accessible AI tools in your area of interest. You don't need to build everything from scratch.
  • Collaborate and Experiment: Treat AI as a creative partner. Try, fail, learn, and try again.
Remember: AI is not the destination—it's the vehicle. Your talent, curiosity, and vision are what drive the journey.

This article itself is an example: AI helped shape the words, but the ideas and direction came from human experience and passion. Start with what you love, and let AI help you make it real.
